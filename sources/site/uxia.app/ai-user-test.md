## Got questions? We have answers

### What is an AI User Test?

### AI usability testing is a way to evaluate digital products using AI testers or synthetic users instead of recruited human participants. These AI testers explore your product, complete tasks, and surface UX friction, usability issues, and behavioral insights in minutes. With Uxia, teams can run AI user tests on prototypes, live products, websites, apps, and complex flows without recruitment, scheduling, or moderation.

### How does AI user testing work?

### AI user testing works by giving synthetic testers a product experience and a specific mission to complete. The testers independently navigate the flow, interact with the interface, reason through each step, and identify points of confusion, friction, or unmet expectations. Uxia then turns those interactions into actionable UX insights, including summarized results, tester behavior, expectations, step-by-step reasoning, and usability scores.

### What are synthetic users?

### Synthetic users are AI-generated testers designed to simulate how real users might interact with a product experience. They can represent different target audiences, goals, behaviors, and expectations, helping teams validate designs faster and earlier in the product development process. Synthetic users are especially useful for UX validation, prototype testing, product flow analysis, and identifying usability issues before launching to real users.

### What kind of insights does Uxia provide?

### Uxia helps teams uncover UX friction, usability issues, user expectations, behavioral patterns, confusion points, and opportunities for product improvement. You can review summarized results for a quick overview, or go deeper into each tester’s step-by-step reasoning, interactions, expectations, and behavior across the flow.

### What can I test with Uxia?

### You can use Uxia to test a wide range of product experiences, including Figma prototypes, static designs, live websites, Lovable, Base44 and similar tools prototypes, authenticated platforms, product flows, dashboards, even unconventional interfaces. AI testers can navigate screens, complete tasks, interact with forms, go through login flows, upload files, and evaluate complex user journeys.

### Can AI testers evaluate Figma prototypes?

### Yes. Uxia can be used to run AI usability tests on Figma prototypes and other design artifacts before development. This helps product and design teams validate flows, identify confusing screens, and uncover usability issues before investing engineering resources. AI prototype testing is especially useful for onboarding flows, checkout experiences, signup flows, dashboards, mobile app concepts, and new feature validation.

### Is AI usability testing the same as unmoderated usability testing?

### AI usability testing is similar to unmoderated usability testing because testers complete tasks independently, without a moderator guiding the session. The main difference is that AI usability testing uses synthetic testers instead of recruited human participants. This makes it much faster to run tests, get feedback, and iterate, because teams do not need to recruit users, schedule sessions, wait for panel responses, manually moderate the research or analyse the recordings.

### Can I combine AI user testing with human usability testing?

### Yes. Uxia supports hybrid UX research by letting teams combine AI usability testing with human feedback. You can use AI testers to identify usability issues quickly, then convert the same test into a shareable human usability test to collect recordings, transcripts, interactions, and feedback from real participants. Everything from one platform.
