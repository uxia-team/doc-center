title: "Responses"
---

The **Responses** tab is the per-respondent view of an AI User Research study. Where [Charts](/charts) aggregates everyone into distributions and themes, Responses lets you read one tester at a time — their profile, and their answer to every question, in their own words.

## Switching between respondents

A respondent switcher lets you move through the testers who took part in your study one by one. Selecting a respondent loads their full set of answers and their profile into the view, so you can read any single participant's study end to end.

## Respondent profile

For the selected respondent, you can review their **profile**, **demographics**, and — when available — their **psychological profile**. This is the same synthetic identity that shaped how they answered: who they are, the background they were built from, and the traits driving their reasoning. It's what turns a raw answer into an interpretable one — you're not just seeing _what_ someone said, but the kind of person who said it.

<Tip>
  When your answers surprise you, the profile is the first place to look. A response that seems off often makes sense once you see the demographic or personality context behind it. Ground respondents in your real users with [Audience Enrichment](/audiences/enrichment) so these profiles reflect the people you're actually building for.
</Tip>

## Reading the answers

Below the profile, you'll see the respondent's answer to **every question** in the study, laid out in order. Each answer includes:

- **The response itself** — the rating, choice, ranking, allocation, or free-text answer, shown in the form that matches the [question type](/question-types).
- **Reasoning** — the thinking behind the answer, so a rating or a pick comes with the _why_ attached rather than a bare number.
- **Attached images** — any visuals the respondent referenced or produced as part of their answer.

Reading a single respondent this way is the closest thing to sitting with one participant and going through your study question by question.

## Following up

If chat is available on your plan and you're signed in, the right-hand panel becomes a **Chat with respondent** drawer. From there you can keep talking to the tester you're viewing — probe a vague answer, ask a follow-up the study didn't cover, or push back on something they said.
