Uxia's results feel like real user research because the underlying methodology is built on real behavioral science — not generic AI text generation. Every test runs through a structured four-step process: defining a human-like audience, launching a simulated session, capturing a think-aloud narrative, and synthesising everything into a prioritised insights report. This page explains exactly what happens at each stage, and why synthetic testers produce findings you can act on with confidence.

## The four-step process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define your target audience">
    Every test begins with an audience, not a task. Before a single synthetic tester interacts with your product, Uxia builds tester profiles that reflect the humans you actually want to reach.

    You specify the demographic and behavioral attributes that matter for your research:

    - **Demographics** — age, gender, country, language, and device preference
    - **Behavioral attributes** — tech literacy, digital shopping habits, prior product experience, and decision-making style
    - **Psychographic data** — motivations, attitudes toward risk, and task orientation

    Uxia draws on proprietary behavioral and psychographic datasets to instantiate each profile as a distinct synthetic person. Two testers assigned the same demographic bracket will still behave differently — because real users in the same bracket do too.

    Once you've configured your audience, Uxia generates a panel of synthetic testers for you to review. You can inspect individual profiles, adjust the mix, and select the testers you want to run your test before you spend a single credit.

    **Audience Enrichment** (Custom plan): if you already have persona research, ICP data, or customer segmentation files, you can upload them directly into Uxia. The platform ingests your data and uses it to instantiate synthetic tester profiles that reflect your actual customer segments — rather than generic demographic brackets. This is particularly valuable for enterprise teams that have invested in audience research and want their test findings to map precisely to known buyer or user segments.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Launch the user test">
    With your audience confirmed, you configure the test itself. Depending on the test type, you provide:

    - **A mission** — the top-level goal the tester is trying to accomplish (e.g. *"Complete a subscription upgrade"*)
    - **A scenario** — optional framing that gives the tester realistic context and intent
    - **Your product** — uploaded designs, a prototype link, or a live URL

    Uxia combines the tester's behavioral profile with your test configuration to initialise a simulated user session. The tester doesn't just navigate your product mechanically — it approaches tasks with the goals, assumptions, and friction tolerances defined by its profile. A low-tech-literacy tester will hesitate at unfamiliar UI patterns; a power user will look for shortcuts and get frustrated by unnecessary steps.

    Tests launch immediately. There is no recruitment pipeline, no scheduling overhead, and no risk of no-shows.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Think-aloud transcripts in minutes">
    As each synthetic tester moves through your product, Uxia captures a continuous think-aloud narrative — a real-time stream of the tester's expressed thoughts, reactions, and decision-making process.

    Think-aloud transcripts surface:

    - **Friction points** — moments where the tester hesitates, backtracks, or expresses confusion
    - **Unmet expectations** — places where the interface doesn't match what the tester anticipated
    - **Emotional tone** — shifts between confidence, frustration, delight, and uncertainty throughout the session

    Because each tester has a distinct profile, transcripts from the same test can reveal how different segments of your audience experience the same flow differently. A path that feels intuitive to a tech-savvy user may completely stall a first-time visitor — and your transcripts will show you exactly where and why.

    Most tests return completed transcripts within minutes of launching.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Actionable insights report">
    Once all tester sessions are complete, Uxia automatically synthesises the findings into a structured insights report. You don't need to read every transcript manually — the report surfaces what matters.

    Each report includes:

    - **Auto-generated findings** — prioritised observations grouped by theme, severity, and affected user segment
    - **Friction maps** — a visual overlay showing where in your product testers encountered the most difficulty
    - **UX scores** with benchmark context:
      - **SUS (System Usability Scale)** — a standardised 0–100 usability score benchmarked against industry averages
      - **SUPR-Q** — a multidimensional score covering usability, credibility, loyalty, and visual appeal for web products
      - **Uxia Optimization Score** — Uxia's proprietary metric that combines task completion rate, friction density, and sentiment into a single number you can track over time

    Findings link directly to the transcript segments that support them, so you can verify any insight with the raw evidence behind it. Share the report with your team, export it for a client presentation, or use it to brief your engineering team on the highest-priority fixes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What makes synthetic testers different from generic AI

Most AI tools that claim to simulate users are, in practice, asking a general-purpose language model to role-play. The output reflects the model's training data, not any specific human population.

Uxia takes a fundamentally different approach. Synthetic tester profiles are instantiated from **proprietary behavioral and psychographic datasets** built to represent how real demographic and attitudinal segments actually behave in digital product contexts. This means:

- **Behavioral variance is real.** Two testers with similar demographics will make different decisions based on their distinct psychographic profiles — just as two real users would.
- **Friction is discovered, not assumed.** Testers don't follow a scripted path. They navigate with goal-directed intent, which means usability problems emerge organically from the interaction rather than being pre-programmed into the test.
- **Findings generalise.** Because profiles are grounded in population-level behavioral data, patterns that appear across multiple testers in the same segment are a reliable signal — not a random artifact of a single model run.

Gartner recognised Uxia as a *Diamond Startup to Watch* in the Synthetic Population and Behavioral Simulation space, validating this approach as a distinct and credible methodology within the UX research landscape.

## Hybrid research: combining AI and human testers

For teams that want maximum confidence, Uxia supports a hybrid research model. You can run an AI test to rapidly identify the most significant friction points, then follow up with a targeted Human Test that focuses real participants on the specific flows or decisions you want to probe more deeply.

This approach gives you the speed and scale of AI testing — run as many sessions as you need, iterate overnight, test edge cases that would be impractical to recruit for — combined with the interpretive richness of human feedback for the questions that matter most. AI tests handle the volume; human tests handle the nuance.
