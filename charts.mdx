title: "Charts"
---

The **Charts** tab is the aggregate view of an AI User Research study. Instead of reading testers one at a time as you do in [Responses](/responses), you see how _everyone_ answered each question at a glance — then drill into any result to see exactly who's behind it.

## Question navigator

A navigator on the left lists every question in the study. Selecting a question brings its results into the center of the view, so you can move through the study question by question without losing your place.

## Reading the charts

Each question is visualized in the way that fits its [type](/question-types). Closed questions — Yes / No, Rating Scale, Multiple Choice, Ranking, and Budget Allocation — render as distributions you can read in a second: how the vote split, where sentiment landed, which option won. Open-ended questions are clustered into **themes**, so a hundred free-text answers become a handful of recurring ideas rather than a wall of text.

## View answers drawer

The charts aren't the end of the trail — they're the way in. Click a **chart segment** (a bar, a slice, an option) or an open-ended **theme**, and a **View answers** drawer opens on the right showing only the respondents behind that specific answer.

From the drawer you can:

- **Inspect just those respondents** — everyone who picked that option or expressed that theme, filtered down from the full set.
- **Clear the filter** to return to the complete list of respondents for the question.
- **Open respondent details** to see the profile and reasoning behind an individual answer.
- **Jump into [respondent chat](/chat-with-testers)** to follow up directly with someone in that segment.

This is what makes the aggregate view trustworthy: every number stays one click away from the individual people who produced it. A surprising spike in the chart is never a dead end — you can always ask _who_ and _why_.
