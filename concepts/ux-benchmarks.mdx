Knowing that users struggle with your product is useful. Knowing *how much* they struggle — relative to industry standards and your own past performance — is what drives confident decisions. Uxia scores every test session using three benchmark frameworks, giving you a consistent, comparable measure of UX quality that you can track over time, present to stakeholders, and use to prioritize where to invest.

## SUS — System Usability Scale

The System Usability Scale is the most widely used standardized usability metric in UX research. It was developed in 1986 and remains the industry benchmark for measuring perceived usability across digital products.

Uxia calculates a SUS score from 10 attitudinal questions that testers respond to during the session. The result is a single number on a **0–100 scale**.

### Interpreting your SUS score

| Score range | Rating | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Above 80** | Excellent | Users find your product highly usable; above the top quartile of measured products |
| **68–80** | Good | Above average usability; most users can accomplish their goals without significant friction |
| **Below 68** | Below average | Usability issues are impacting the experience; improvement is needed to meet user expectations |

A score of 68 is the empirical average across thousands of measured products. Use it as your baseline: below 68 means your product has meaningful usability debt, and each point above it represents measurable improvement.

SUS scores are particularly useful for communicating with stakeholders who aren't immersed in UX detail — a single number with an established industry benchmark is easier to act on than a list of qualitative observations.

## SUPR-Q — Standardized User Experience Percentile Rank Questionnaire

Where SUS focuses narrowly on usability, SUPR-Q gives you a broader view of the user experience across four dimensions:

| Dimension | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **Usability** | How easy and efficient the product is to use |
| **Trust** | Whether users feel confident and secure using it |
| **Loyalty** | The likelihood of users returning or recommending the product |
| **Appearance** | Users' aesthetic perception and visual quality impression |

SUPR-Q results are expressed as a **percentile rank** — your score tells you how your product compares to the full dataset of measured products. A 70th percentile score means your product outperforms 70% of benchmarked products on that dimension.

This format makes SUPR-Q especially useful for identifying *which* dimension is dragging your overall experience down. A product can score well on usability but poorly on trust — which points to a very different set of fixes than the reverse.

## Uxia Optimization Score

The Uxia Optimization Score is Uxia's proprietary benchmark. While SUS and SUPR-Q measure against broad, cross-industry datasets, the Uxia Optimization Score benchmarks your product **against others in your specific industry vertical**.

This means your score accounts for the norms and expectations of your actual competitive context. A score that looks average on a universal scale might be exceptional for your category — or a score that looks decent universally might indicate you're falling behind category leaders.

The Uxia Optimization Score is recalculated with each test, so it reflects both your latest results and any shifts in industry benchmarks over time. It's the most actionable signal for teams who want to know not just whether their UX is good in absolute terms, but whether it's competitive.

## How to use your benchmark scores

Benchmark scores become most valuable when you use them consistently across your development cycle:

**Track improvement across iterations**
Run a test before and after a redesign sprint. A rising SUS score confirms that your changes improved usability — not just that they looked better in a design review.

**Make the case for UX investment**
A below-average SUS score or a low SUPR-Q trust percentile is a quantified, defensible argument for allocating resources to UX work. It speaks the language of business decisions more clearly than qualitative findings alone.

**Prioritize by severity**
Use benchmark scores alongside the Insights Report to stack-rank improvements. Issues that are dragging your score below the 68 SUS threshold should take precedence over refinements to a product already performing in the excellent range.

**Compare across product areas**
Run separate tests on different flows or sections of your product. Benchmark scores let you identify which areas are underperforming relative to others — and focus your effort where it matters most.

<Tip>
  Run tests at consistent intervals — for example, at the end of each sprint or before and after each major release. Benchmark scores measured at a single point in time tell you where you stand; scores measured over time tell you whether you're moving in the right direction.
</Tip>
