After your test completes, Uxia gives you two layers of results: full think-aloud transcripts from each individual tester, and an auto-generated Insights Report that synthesizes findings across the entire session. Together, they give you both the granular detail to understand *why* something is broken and the structured summary to decide *what* to fix first.

## Transcripts

Every synthetic tester produces a complete transcript of their session. This isn't a log of clicks and page views — it's a running narration of the tester's experience as it happened.

### What a transcript contains

Each transcript captures the full think-aloud stream from a single tester's session:

- **Actions taken** — what the tester clicked, scrolled past, ignored, or interacted with, in sequence
- **Reasoning** — why the tester made each decision, in their own words
- **Hesitation moments** — where the tester paused, second-guessed themselves, or re-read something
- **Confusion and frustration** — explicit moments where something didn't work as expected or matched the tester's mental model
- **Expectations** — what the tester anticipated would happen, and whether reality matched

### How to read transcripts

Transcripts are displayed per tester, so you can follow one person's complete journey from start to finish. This individual view is valuable for spotting patterns unique to a specific user type — a tester with low tech literacy struggling at a step that high-literacy testers sailed through, for example.

Look for recurring friction points across multiple transcripts: if three out of five testers hesitate at the same screen, that's a signal worth acting on regardless of what the summary says.

<Tip>
  Don't skip the individual transcripts in favor of reading only the Insights Report. The summary tells you what the problems are — the transcripts tell you *how* users experience them. That qualitative texture is what separates a useful fix from a patch that misses the root cause.
</Tip>

## The Insights Report

Once all testers complete the session, Uxia automatically generates an Insights Report. You don't need to tag, code, or manually analyze the transcripts — the report is ready as soon as the last session finishes.

### What the Insights Report includes

**UX friction summary**
A high-level overview of the most significant usability issues found across all tester sessions, described in plain language with context about where and how they surfaced.

**Categorized issues**
Issues are grouped into four categories to help you route findings to the right team:

| Category | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **Usability** | Interaction problems, confusing flows, broken affordances |
| **Navigation** | Information architecture issues, lost users, dead ends |
| **Copy & Messaging** | Unclear labels, confusing instructions, mismatched expectations |
| **Trust & Credibility** | Signals that created hesitation or eroded confidence |

**Prioritized recommendations**
Each identified issue comes with an actionable recommendation. Recommendations are prioritized by the severity and frequency of the friction observed, so you can work through them in order of impact rather than guessing at priority.

### Sharing your report

Reports are shareable via a link, making it straightforward to bring findings into a team review, hand off to a developer, or include in a stakeholder presentation. No Uxia account is required to view a shared report.

## AI User Research: additional outputs

When you use **AI User Research**, testers respond to structured questions rather than navigating a product. The results layer adds outputs specific to research sessions:

**Answer patterns**
Uxia identifies recurring themes and patterns across tester responses, surfacing what multiple testers said or felt in common — without you having to manually compare each answer.

**Summaries per question**
Each research question gets a synthesized summary of how testers responded, distilling the key points from across the full panel.

**Directional insights**
High-level takeaways that point toward decisions — what the data suggests about user needs, preferences, attitudes, or unmet expectations.

### Chat with testers after the session

In AI User Research, you can continue the conversation after the session ends. Ask any tester a follow-up question — dig deeper into an answer, probe a specific topic, or explore something that came up during analysis. The tester responds in character, drawing on the same behavioral and psychographic profile they used during the session.

This lets you treat the session as a starting point rather than a fixed artifact, and pursue the threads that matter most to your research question.
