Audience Enrichment lets you build synthetic testers directly from your own user data. Instead of relying on Uxia's default tester pool, you upload the audience definitions you already have — personas, ICP documents, segment descriptions, behavioral research — and Uxia generates testers that reflect *your* users, not a generalized population.

## Why default testers aren't always enough

Uxia's standard synthetic testers cover a broad range of realistic user profiles, and for many tests that's exactly what you need. But if your product serves a well-defined, specific audience, a generic panel can miss the nuances that matter most.

A B2B SaaS tool built for compliance officers at mid-market financial firms has a very different user than a consumer fitness app. When your audience has distinct workflows, vocabulary, risk tolerances, or trust patterns, your test results are only as good as the match between your testers and your real users. Audience Enrichment closes that gap.

## What you can upload

Uxia accepts a range of document and data formats to inform your synthetic audience:

- **Persona documents** — narrative or structured persona definitions, including goals, frustrations, behaviors, and quotes
- **ICP definitions** — firmographic and psychographic descriptions of your ideal customer profile
- **Audience segment descriptions** — segment breakdowns by behavior, lifecycle stage, use case, or any other dimension you use internally
- **Behavioral data** — anonymized usage patterns, onboarding analytics, support ticket themes, or session recording summaries
- **Internal research docs** — previous interview summaries, survey findings, usability study reports, or customer feedback compilations

The more specific and detailed your inputs, the more precisely Uxia can configure your synthetic testers.

## How Uxia uses your data

Uxia doesn't simply quote your documents back as tester behavior. Your uploaded data is combined with Uxia's behavioral frameworks and public demographic information to produce tester profiles that are both grounded in your audience reality and behaviorally coherent.

Here's how the process works:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload your audience data">
    Add one or more documents to your test configuration — persona files, ICP definitions, segment data, or research outputs. Uxia accepts PDF, DOCX, and plain text formats.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Uxia generates candidate profiles">
    Uxia's enrichment engine analyzes your inputs and generates a set of synthetic tester profiles tailored to the audience you've described. Each profile includes demographics, behavioral patterns, and psychographic traits derived from your data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and select your testers">
    You see the generated profiles before anything runs. Review each candidate, inspect their attributes, and select the testers you want in your session. You're in control of who runs your test.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch your test">
    Your selected enriched testers run the session. Transcripts and insights reflect how your actual target audience would experience your product.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The review and selection step

Before your test launches, Uxia presents you with the full set of generated tester profiles. This step matters: you can confirm that the profiles align with your intent, spot any that feel off-target, and hand-pick the lineup that best represents your audience.

You're never locked into an auto-assigned panel. The review step exists so that your judgment — not just the algorithm — shapes who tests your product.
