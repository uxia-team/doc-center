Synthetic testers are AI-generated research participants that simulate how real users think, navigate, and react when interacting with your product. Instead of recruiting and scheduling sessions with human participants, Uxia lets you run tests instantly — with testers that reflect the specific demographics, behaviors, and psychographic traits of your target audience.

## What makes Uxia's synthetic testers different

Most AI-generated content is produced by general-purpose language models trained to predict plausible text. Uxia's synthetic testers are built differently. Each tester is grounded in **proprietary behavioral and psychographic datasets** that model how people with specific backgrounds, habits, and motivations actually use digital products.

This means a 22-year-old student in Brazil with low tech literacy doesn't just say different things than a 45-year-old IT manager in Germany — they _behave_ differently. They hesitate in different places, trust different signals, and interpret UI patterns through different mental models. That distinction is what makes synthetic testers a genuine research tool rather than a text generation exercise.

## What a tester profile includes

Every synthetic tester is defined by a layered profile that covers three dimensions:

**Demographics**

- Age and gender
- Country and language
- Education level
- Occupation and industry
- Tech literacy (low / moderate / high)
- Device and platform preferences

**Behavioral patterns**

- How they scan and navigate interfaces
- Their tolerance for friction and ambiguity
- Their typical decision-making pace
- Prior exposure to similar products or workflows

**Psychographic traits**

- Attitudes toward trust, privacy, and data sharing
- Motivation drivers (efficiency, curiosity, caution, social proof)
- Risk appetite and willingness to explore unfamiliar UI patterns
- Emotional responses to errors and unexpected outcomes

## How testers behave during a session

During a test, each synthetic tester works through your product or prototype while **thinking aloud** — narrating their actions, expectations, and reactions in real time. You'll see them:

- Describe what they're looking at and what they expect to happen next
- Express hesitation when something doesn't match their mental model
- Call out moments of confusion, frustration, or surprise
- Explain _why_ they clicked (or didn't click) something
- React to copy, visuals, and layout as they encounter them

This continuous narration is captured in full as a [transcript](/concepts/transcripts-and-insights), giving you a window into the reasoning behind every interaction — not just the outcomes.

## How to configure your testers

You control who tests your product. Before launching any test, you can shape your tester lineup in several ways:

**Upload a persona document** If you have existing persona definitions, upload them directly. Uxia uses the document to configure testers that reflect your described user types — their goals, frustrations, and behavioral tendencies.

**Target by ICP or segment** Define your ideal customer profile or a specific audience segment using descriptive criteria. Uxia matches testers from its behavioral dataset to fit that profile.

**Apply demographic filters** Set specific parameters — age range, country, gender, tech literacy level, industry — to narrow the tester pool to the audience that matters for this test.

For advanced customization using your own proprietary audience data, see [Audience Enrichment](/concepts/audience-enrichment).

## Example tester profile

To make this concrete, here's what a synthetic tester profile looks like in practice:

> **Maya R.** — 34 years old, Marketing Manager, London, UK
>
> Maya uses SaaS tools daily across project management, CRM, and analytics platforms. She has moderate tech literacy — comfortable with mainstream software but unlikely to explore settings menus or read documentation. She moves quickly through interfaces and relies heavily on familiar visual patterns. Maya is cautious about sharing personal or company data and will abandon a flow if she encounters an unclear permissions prompt. She responds well to social proof and recognizable brand signals.

When Maya runs your test, her behavioral model shapes where she pauses, what she reads carefully, and what she skips entirely — producing a session that reflects how a real user matching her profile would actually experience your product.
