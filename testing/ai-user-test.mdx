An AI User Test lets you validate your product with synthetic testers — AI-generated users who independently explore your design, attempt real goals, and narrate their reasoning as they go. You get the depth of unmoderated usability testing without scheduling, recruiting, or waiting for participants.

## What you can test

Uxia's AI testers work across a wide range of formats and environments:

- Figma prototypes (interactive and static)
- Live websites and web applications
- Staging and pre-production environments
- Gated products (provide credentials to unlock authenticated flows)
- Complex multi-step flows and non-standard interfaces
- Partially built or in-progress products

## How to set up an AI User Test

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new test">
    From your Uxia dashboard, select **AI User Test** and give your test a name. Choose the type of design you're testing — a Figma prototype, a live URL, or a static design file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define your synthetic audience">
    Configure the profile of your synthetic testers. Set demographics (age range, location, occupation), psychographic attributes (tech-savviness, domain familiarity, attitudes), or upload a persona file to construct testers who match your real users.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your mission and scenario">
    Give testers a **mission** — the specific goal they need to accomplish — and a **scenario** that frames the context of their visit. A clear mission produces focused, actionable results. For example: _"You're looking for a project management tool for a remote team of five. Explore this app and sign up for a plan."_
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach your designs">
    Upload your Figma prototype link, paste a live URL, or provide credentials for a gated product. If you're testing an authenticated flow, add the login details testers will need to access it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the test">
    Review your configuration and launch. Synthetic testers begin immediately — no scheduling or participant coordination required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and share your report">
    Once testing is complete, open your report to explore transcripts, friction points, and scores. Download a shareable insights report to distribute findings to your team or stakeholders.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How a test session works

Each AI tester receives your mission and scenario, then navigates your product independently — just as a real user would. While exploring, each tester thinks aloud: Uxia captures a full transcript of their reasoning, hesitations, and decisions at every step. Every action is recorded, giving you a complete picture of how your design performs under realistic conditions.

## What you get in the report

After your test completes, Uxia produces a structured report covering:

- **UX friction summary** — the most significant barriers and pain points discovered across all testers
- **Per-tester transcripts** — step-by-step think-aloud recordings showing exactly what each tester thought and did
- **Behavioral patterns** — recurring confusion points, drop-off moments, and navigation trends
- **Standardized metrics** — SUS (System Usability Scale) scores, SUPR-Q equivalents, and the Uxia Optimization Score
- **Industry benchmarks** — see how your product compares against established baselines for your category

## Combining AI and human testing

AI testing gives you fast, scalable signal — but you can go deeper by layering in real human participants. Any AI User Test can be extended into a [Human Test](/testing/human-test), where real users follow the same mission and scenario. Both sets of results appear in the same platform, so you can compare synthetic and human findings side by side.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is a synthetic user?">
    A synthetic user is an AI-generated tester that simulates how a real person interacts with your product. Each synthetic user has a defined profile, goals, and behavioral tendencies. They navigate independently, make decisions based on what they see, and think aloud throughout — producing realistic interaction data without the need for human recruitment.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How is this different from traditional unmoderated testing?">
    AI User Tests work the same way as unmoderated usability testing — testers complete tasks on their own, without a moderator present. The key difference is that synthetic testers are available instantly. You don't need to recruit participants, wait for completion windows, or manage scheduling. You can run a full round of testing in minutes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I test a product that requires a login?">
    Yes. When setting up your test, you can provide credentials for gated or authenticated areas of your product. Synthetic testers will use those credentials to access the relevant flows, so you can validate onboarding, dashboards, checkout, and any other post-login experience.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How many synthetic testers should I use?">
    For most usability tests, five testers surface the majority of significant UX issues. You can run more testers to increase coverage, explore edge cases, or segment by audience profile. Uxia lets you configure the number and characteristics of testers before you launch.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
