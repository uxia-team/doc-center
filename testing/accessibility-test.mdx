Catching accessibility issues in design is dramatically cheaper and faster than fixing them in code — and research shows that 67% of accessibility problems originate at the design stage. Uxia's Accessibility Test automatically reviews your designs against WCAG 2.2 standards and delivers a clear, actionable report so your team can resolve issues before a single line of code is written.

## What Uxia checks

Uxia evaluates your designs against the full range of **WCAG 2.2 AA and AAA criteria**, including:

- Color contrast ratios for text, icons, and interactive elements
- Touch and click target sizes
- Focus order and keyboard navigation paths
- Text alternatives for non-text content
- Form labels, error states, and input instructions
- Motion, animation, and timing controls
- Visual indicators for state changes and interactive elements

If you provide a live URL alongside your design file, Uxia can run additional checks that require a rendered environment — expanding the coverage of your report.

## What the report includes

Your accessibility report gives you everything you need to understand, prioritize, and fix issues:

- **Passed criteria** — a clear record of what already meets the standard, so you know what not to change
- **Failed criteria** — specific violations with references to the relevant WCAG success criterion
- **Review-needed items** — criteria that require human judgment or context beyond automated checking
- **Actionable checklist** — a prioritized list of fixes your team can work through directly
- **Plain-language explanations** — every issue is described in plain English, not just WCAG codes, so designers and developers can understand the impact without specialist knowledge
- **Design vs. code scope assignment** — each issue is flagged as a design-layer fix or a development-layer fix, so the right person gets the right task

## How to run an accessibility test

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload your designs or provide a URL">
    Connect your Figma file or paste a live URL. If you have both, providing the URL enables additional automated checks on top of the design review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the test">
    Uxia automatically evaluates your designs against WCAG 2.2 AA and AAA criteria. No manual configuration is required — the check runs end to end.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your report">
    Open your report to see passed, failed, and review-needed criteria alongside plain-language explanations and a prioritized checklist.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign and fix issues">
    Use the design vs. code scope labels to route each fix to the right team member. Work through the checklist and re-run the test to verify improvements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share your report">
    Download or share your accessibility report with your broader team, including developers and stakeholders, so everyone has visibility into what needs to be addressed and who owns each fix.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Important limitation

<Warning>
  Uxia's Accessibility Test helps your team catch issues early in the design process, but it does not replace a formal legal accessibility audit or specialist certification. Automated tools can detect a subset of WCAG criteria — some issues require manual expert review, assistive technology testing, and disabled user involvement to fully evaluate. If you have legal compliance requirements, engage a qualified accessibility specialist in addition to using Uxia.
</Warning>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does Uxia check both AA and AAA WCAG 2.2 criteria?">
    Yes. Uxia checks against both AA and AAA success criteria. AA is the widely adopted compliance baseline required by most legal standards. AAA criteria go further and represent best-practice accessibility. Your report clearly distinguishes between the two levels so you can prioritize accordingly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need a live URL, or can I test Figma designs only?">
    You can run an accessibility test on Figma designs alone. Providing a live URL expands the scope of the test by enabling checks that require a rendered page — such as dynamic focus behavior and some interactive state evaluations — but it's optional.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who should use the accessibility report — designers or developers?">
    Both. The report uses the design vs. code scope labels to route each issue to the right person. Design-layer issues (color choices, spacing, component structure) go to designers. Development-layer issues (ARIA attributes, semantic HTML, keyboard handling) go to developers. Everyone gets a task list relevant to their role.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I re-run the test after making fixes?">
    Yes. You can re-run your accessibility test at any point to check your progress, verify that issues have been resolved, and catch any new problems introduced by design changes. Running the test iteratively throughout your design process is the most effective way to ship accessible products.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
