---
title: "Chat with testers"
---

A study gives you every respondent's answers, but sometimes an answer raises a new question. **Chat with testers** lets you keep talking to any respondent after the study runs — probing a vague response, asking something the study didn't cover, or pushing back on what they said. It's the flexibility of a one-on-one interview at the scale of a survey, and because the testers are synthetic, there's no scheduling or recruiting to arrange.

## Starting a chat

You can open a chat with a respondent from either results view:

- **From [Responses](/responses)** — when chat is available and you're signed in, the right-hand panel of the per-respondent view becomes a **Chat with respondent** drawer for the tester you're reading.
- **From [Charts](/charts)** — filter to a chart segment or theme, open a respondent from the **View answers** drawer, and jump straight into a conversation with them.

Either way, the chat is scoped to a single respondent, and it carries their profile and their study answers as context — so you're continuing a conversation, not starting from scratch.

## Requirements

<Note>
  Chat is available on plans that include it, and only when you're signed in as an authenticated viewer. If you don't see the chat panel, check that you're logged in and that your plan includes the feature.
</Note>

## What to ask

Chat earns its keep when you use it to go past what the study captured:

- **Probe a thin answer** — "You rated onboarding a 2 — what specifically got in your way?"
- **Chase a theme** — follow up with the respondents behind a recurring open-ended theme to understand what's really driving it.
- **Pressure-test a claim** — push back on a confident response and see whether it holds.
- **Explore the unasked** — ask the question you only thought of once the results were in front of you.

Because the respondent responds in character, grounded in the same profile that shaped their original answers, the follow-up stays consistent with who they are. To make those personas reflect your actual users, build your audience with [Audience Enrichment](/audiences/enrichment).
