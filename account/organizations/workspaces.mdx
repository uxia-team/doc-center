Each workspace inside your organization is an isolated hub for tests, audiences, reports, and members. The **Workspaces** tab on the Organization page is where owners manage those workspaces at the organization level, and inside any individual workspace your team runs tests and collaborates on research.

## Managing Workspaces from the Organization Page

The **Workspaces** tab lets organization owners administer all workspaces in one place.

Available actions:

- View all workspaces in the organization.
- See how many workspaces are currently in use compared with your organization limit.
- Create a new workspace with the **Create workspace** button.
- Rename an existing workspace.
- See how many members belong to each workspace.

<Warning>
  If your organization has reached its workspace limit, new workspaces cannot be created until the limit is increased. [Contact us](mailto:hello@uxia.app) to raise it.
</Warning>

<Note>
  A **Delete** option is shown, but workspace deletion is not currently handled directly from this page. Contact Uxia support if you need a workspace removed.
</Note>

## What Each Workspace Includes

Inside a workspace you'll find everything you need to plan, run, and analyze user research:

- **Test Library** — every test you've created or run, organized chronologically. Open any past test to review its report, transcripts, or tester sessions.
- **Audiences** — saved tester profiles and demographic filters you can reuse across studies. Build an audience once and apply it to any future test.
- **Reports** — AI-generated insights reports tied to each completed test. Access, share, or export reports directly from the workspace dashboard.
- **Team Members** — invite colleagues to your workspace so they can collaborate on tests, view reports, and contribute to research planning (available on the Custom plan).

## Sharing Reports

After a test completes, Uxia generates an insights report summarizing findings, patterns, and recommendations. You can share this report with anyone — including stakeholders who don't have a Uxia account — in two ways:

- **Shareable link** — generate a view-only link from the report page and send it directly to teammates or clients.
- **Export** — download the report to share as a file or include in a presentation or research repository.

Both options are available from the report view inside your workspace.

## Generating a Human Test Link

If you've already run an AI test and want to follow up with real human participants, you can generate a shareable Human Test link directly from that existing test. This turns your AI-designed study into a live, recruitable session without rebuilding the test from scratch.

For full details on setting up hybrid research flows, see the [Hybrid Research guide](/guides/hybrid-research).

## Team Access and Collaboration

On the Custom plan, you can add teammates to your workspace and collaborate in real time. Team members can:

- View and comment on test results and reports
- Access shared audiences and apply them to new tests
- Launch tests within the shared workspace environment

To manage workspace-level membership, use the [People tab](/account/organizations/people) on the Organization page to invite users and assign them to specific workspaces.

<Note>
  Workspace customization — including branded reports, custom workspace settings, and team collaboration — requires the Custom plan. [Contact hello@uxia.app](mailto:hello@uxia.app) or visit [uxia.app](https://uxia.app) to learn more about upgrading.
</Note>

## Custom Workspace (Custom Plan)

The Custom plan includes a fully branded workspace tailored to your organization:

- **Branded reports** with your company's look and feel, ready to share with clients or internal stakeholders
- **Custom workspace settings** configured to match your team's research workflow
- **Priority support** so your team can move faster and get help when it matters

Custom workspace settings are configured during onboarding with the Uxia team.

## Tips for Staying Organized

<Tip>
  Name each test with a clear structure — for example, include the product area, the test type, and the date (e.g., `Checkout Flow – AI User Test – 2025-06`). This makes it easy to find past studies as your test library grows. Use separate audiences to group tester profiles by segment, persona, or client so you can reuse them across studies without rebuilding filters each time.
</Tip>
