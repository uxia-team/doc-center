Uxia is built to handle sensitive research assets with care. Whether you're uploading design files, sharing live URLs, capturing human participant recordings, or testing behind authenticated flows, every piece of data you bring into the platform is processed with security and privacy as a baseline — not an afterthought. This page explains exactly how your data is handled at each stage of the testing process.

## Your Design and URL Data

When you upload a design file or provide a URL for an AI test, Uxia processes that asset securely to generate and run the tester session. Here's what that means in practice:

- **Your data is never used to train models.** Designs, URLs, and test content you submit are not used to improve or fine-tune any AI model — for Uxia or any third party.
- **Your data is not shared with other users or organizations.** Each workspace is isolated; your test assets are only accessible to you and the teammates you explicitly invite.
- **Assets are stored only as long as needed.** Uploaded files and URLs are retained for the duration of the test and to support your ongoing access to results and reports. They are not kept indefinitely beyond what is required to deliver the service.

## Participant Recordings (Human Test)

When you run a Human Test, Uxia captures session recordings of real participants interacting with your product or prototype. These recordings are:

- **Stored securely** within the Uxia platform and accessible only within your workspace
- **Not shared with third parties** — recordings remain private to your organization
- **Available for review and analysis** inside your workspace dashboard, alongside the AI-generated insights for that test

Recordings give you the qualitative depth to complement the AI-synthesized findings and are retained in your workspace for as long as your account is active.

## Enterprise Security Features

For organizations with stricter identity and access management requirements, the Custom plan includes enterprise-grade security controls.

### Single Sign-On (SSO)

SSO lets your team log in to Uxia using your existing company identity provider — such as Okta, Azure AD, or Google Workspace. This means:

- Team members authenticate through a system your IT team already manages and trusts
- You maintain centralized control over who has access to Uxia
- Offboarding a team member from your identity provider automatically revokes their Uxia access

### SCIM Provisioning

SCIM (System for Cross-domain Identity Management) automates user provisioning and deprovisioning across your organization. When you add or remove a user in your identity provider, that change is reflected in Uxia automatically — no manual seat management required.

<Note>
  SSO and SCIM are available on the Custom plan. Contact [hello@uxia.app](mailto:hello@uxia.app) to configure enterprise authentication for your organization.
</Note>

## Credentials for Gated and Authenticated Tests

If the product or area you're testing sits behind a login screen, you can provide credentials so that AI testers can access protected flows. When you do:

- Credentials are used solely to run the tester session and are not stored beyond that session
- Uxia does not retain, log, or share login credentials after the test completes
- You remain in control of those credentials and can rotate them after each test run if your security policy requires it

This makes it safe to test onboarding flows, dashboards, account settings, or any other authenticated experience without exposing long-lived credentials.
